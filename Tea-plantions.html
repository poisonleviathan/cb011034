What is a Tea Region

As the altitudes and temperature of the region are major factors to the flavor profile of tea, Ceylon tea is divided into several categories according to where it is cultivated.

  • High-grown tea plantations
  • Mid-grown tea plantations
  • Low-grown tea plantations

Tea Regions In Sri Lanka

High-grown tea plantations in Sri Lanka


High grown tea may be found at heights of above 6000 feet in Sri Lanka's central highlands. The country's primary tea-growing regions are Nuwara Eliya, Uda Pussellawa, and Dimbulla.


  1. Nuwara Eliya Tea Plantation

    2. One of the major tea growing districts in the country since its first tea yields in the 1870s plantations. This small town, fondly known as the little England, produces some of the most relished tea


  2. Uda Pussellawa Tea Plantation

    3. While being a neighbour to Nuwara Eliya, the climate of the Uda Pussallawa region is quite different due to its location. The tea itself is slightly darker and appears more potent in flavour compared to the tea found in the Nuwara Eliya region.



  3. Dimbulla Tea Plantation

    4. You are in Dimbulla tea region if you are traveling from Hatton to Nuwara Eliya. This tea has a golden-orange colour and a clear, fresh flavor.




Mid-grown tea plantations in Sri Lanka


Mid grown tea is grown at elevations ranging from 2000 to 4000 feet above sea level and is most commonly found in Sri Lanka's Central and Uva provinces.


  1. Badulla Tea Plantation

    2. Badulla is a tea region located in the Uva province of Sri Lanka. The tea that you find in Badulla generally has a mellow, smooth taste which is a unique flavour profile that you do not find anywhere else in the country.



  2. Kandy Tea Plantation

    3. The hill capital of Kandy that is well known for the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is a popular destination among travellers to Sri Lanka. Kandy is another location for mid-grown tea with its bright and robust infusions.




Low-grown tea plantations in Sri Lanka


Tea plantations falling to the low-grown tea category may be found in the Sabaragamuwa and Southern regions of Sri Lanka at elevations not exceeding 2000 ft.


  1. Sabaragamuwa Tea Plantation

    2. All the way from the west of Kandy to Udawalawae, passing through Horton Plains, you may see the tea-growing regions in Sabaragamuwa. Tea in this area grows at about 600m above sea level. Due to the long period of sunshine and dry weather here, this tea variety presents a noticeably different aroma with a hint of caramel.



  2. Ruhuna Tea Plantation

    3. Another region famous for low-grown tea with its full and strong flavours, Ruhana offers intensely black and withered looking tea leaves due to its climate and topography. You can find this tea in the southern province close to the coast.
